Methodological Framework and Cost Estimation Principles for the Smart Grid vs. Power Plant Decision-Making Calculator

1. Introduction

This document describes the reasons, mathematical equations and data needed to develop a tool for comparing an upgrade to an existing grid with building a new power plant. This model considers capital expenditure, previously gained revenue, loss savings and measures investment option performance by calculating ROI and NPV. Prices are adjusted to account for differences in economies among countries.

2. Formula Derivation and Assumptions

2.1. Cost of Building a New Power Plant

Formula:

Cplant = Pnew × Ctype × RCAF

The formula allows you to estimate how much it would cost to build a new power plant. Pnew shows the total extra capacity, the type of plant (solar, coal, nuclear, etc.) is shown by Ctype and RCAF makes adjustments for regional economy. As labor, logistics and material prices for a power plant differ depending on the region, the RCAF ensures that the calculation reflects the costs in that region. For example, RCAF greater than 1 is needed in Sub-Saharan Africa since their infrastructure is not as good as in North America.

Where:
  • Pnew = additional power capacity required (in MW)

  • Ctype = cost per MW based on power technology

  • RCAF = Regional Cost Adjustment Factor (index adjusting for local cost variability)

    2. Cost of Upgrading to a Smart Grid

    Formula:

    Csmart = Cinfra + (Nmeters × Cmeters)

    This formular calculates the cost of upgrading the old grid to a smart grid. The fixed cost of building the infrastructure (including control systems) for the smart grid is Cinfra and the cost to supply smart meters to customers is found by multiplying the Nmeters and Cmeters. It matters because forming a smart grid requires both initial construction and distribution of devices to individuals and the cost increases with the number of people involved.

    Where:
  • Cinfra = infrastructure cost (communication system, sensors, software)

  • Nmeters = number of customers

  • Cmeters = cost per smart meter

    • 3. Annual Revenue from a New Power Plant

    Formula:

    Rplant = Pnew × H × Ttariff

    This formular calculates the yearly income from a built power plant. The new capacity is called Pnew, H represents the total operating hours in a year and Ttariff shows the hourly cost of electricity at the selling point. Once you multiply these variables, you obtain the electricity sold and the amount of money made from it. With this calculation, investors see how much income the plant is predicted to generate as it is normally put to use.

    Where:
  • H = operating hours per year

  • Ttariff = tariff per kilowatt-hour

    • 4. Annual Savings from Smart Grid

    Formula:

    Ssmart = Pnew × H × Ttariff × Ssavings

    This formular calculates the yearly savings after using a smart grid. Pnew means current electricity demand, H signifies the annual number of operating hours and Ttariff is the unit electricity tariff. The smart grid is expected to stop a percentage of losses (both technical and non-technical) and this is shown by the variable Ssavings. By using smart grids, energy theft is lessened, as are technical losses and inefficiencies which helps save money.

    Where:
  • Pnew = current demand

  • Ssavings = percentage of loss reduction from smart monitoring (typical: 5–15%)

    • 5. Investment Evaluation Metrics

    4.1. Return on Investment (ROI)

    Formula:

  • New Plant: ROIplant = (Rplant / Cplant) × 100

  • Smart Grid: ROIsmart = (Ssmart / Csmart) × 100

    • This shows profitability based on yearly return relative to initial investment.

    5.2. Net Present Value (NPV)

    Formula:

    NPV = ∑ (Rt / (1 + r)^t) - C

    Where:
  • Rt = revenue or savings per year

  • r = discount rate (default: 8%)

  • L = lifespan (default: 20 years)

  • C = capital cost (Cplant or Csmart)

    • This accounts for time value of money to determine long-term investment merit.

    6. Application of Formulas in the Web Tool

    Users select:

    Technology type → determines Ctype
    Region → determines RCAF
    Enter demand, cost inputs, tariff, lifespan, and discount rate

    The tool calculates:

    Total capital cost for both options
    Annual financial impact
    ROI and NPV

    A final decision rule based on:

    If NPVsmart is Greater NPVplant and ROIsmart is greater ROIplant → Smart Grid
    Otherwise → Build a New Power Plant
